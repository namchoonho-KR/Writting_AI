[ACADEMIC PAPERS]

1. Artificial Intelligence-Assisted Academic Writing:
   Recommendations for Ethical Use
   Source : PMC / PubMed (2025)
   URL    : https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12007126/
   Summary: Lays out concrete ethical recommendations for researchers
            using AI in academic writing. Covers disclosure requirements,
            authorship criteria (AI cannot be an author), the necessity
            of human vetting for accuracy, and how to document AI use
            in Methods or Acknowledgements sections.

2. Practical Considerations and Ethical Implications of Using
   Artificial Intelligence in Writing Scientific Manuscripts
   Source : PMC (2025)
   URL    : https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11838153/
   Summary: Reviews practical scenarios where researchers use AI in
            manuscript preparation and maps each to its ethical
            implications. Addresses hallucination risk, fabricated
            references, plagiarism detection limitations, and the
            growing policy landscape across publishers.

3. The Impact of Generative AI on Academic Integrity of Authentic
   Assessments within a Higher Education Context
   Source : British Journal of Educational Technology (BJET), 2025
            Kofinas et al.
   URL    : https://bera-journals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/bjet.13585
   Summary: Empirical study in higher education examining how generative
            AI challenges traditional notions of academic integrity.
            Proposes a reframing of assessment design and institutional
            policy to balance AI's benefits against risks of misuse,
            ghost-writing, and erosion of deep learning.

[BLOGS / GUIDES]

1. Ethical Use Cases of AI in Academic Writing:
   A 2025 Guide for Students and Researchers
   Source : Thesify Blog
   URL    : https://www.thesify.ai/blog/ethical-use-cases-of-ai-in-academic-writing-a-2025-guide-for-students-and-researchers
   Summary: Practical guide distinguishing acceptable AI use (grammar
            correction, structural feedback, brainstorming) from
            unacceptable use (full draft generation, fabricating
            citations). Provides decision-tree-style guidance for
            navigating institutional policies.

2. New Ethical Framework to Help Navigate Use of AI
   in Academic Research
   Source : University of Oxford News (2024-11-13)
   URL    : https://www.ox.ac.uk/news/2024-11-13-new-ethical-framework-help-navigate-use-ai-academic-research
   Summary: Oxford University announces a principled ethical framework
            for AI use in research. Identifies three core criteria:
            human vetting and guaranteeing accuracy, ensuring substantial
            human intellectual contribution, and transparent disclosure
            of AI involvement.

3. Ethical AI Writing Guidelines 2025: Best Practices for Integrity
   Source : Hastewire Blog
   URL    : https://hastewire.com/blog/ethical-ai-writing-guidelines-2025-best-practices-for-integrity
   Summary: Accessible overview of AI writing ethics aimed at a broad
            audience. Covers best practices for transparency, avoiding
            AI-generated misinformation, combining AI tools with human
            critical thinking, and maintaining scholarly voice and
            accountability.
